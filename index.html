Four of Key West's most celebrated chefs.
One 1962 landmark.

Each concept retains its own identity, its own name, its own story within the building. The Reserve is the umbrella, never the override.

01

Thai Island
/ MisoHappy

Sushi · Regional Thai · Good Spirits · Pan-Asian

A Key West institution since 2008, Thai Island began at Garrison Bight Marina before evolving into MisoHappy at 504 Southard — practically next door to The Reserve. What defines this kitchen is not just the food but the philosophy behind it.

The chef grows her own Thai herbs and spices on the island. Lemongrass. Basil. Papaya. Ingredients that most restaurants import from a supplier, she grows herself, on a tropical island, for the guests she serves. This is not Thai food from a box. This is an immigrant chef who planted a garden so she could cook the way she learned to cook at home.

Signature

Fresh herbs grown on-island by the chef · Sushi, Regional Thai & Pan-Asian · Chef name to be confirmed by ownership

Thai Island
/ MisoHappy
Sushi · Thai · Good Spirits
02

OnlyWood

Neapolitan Pizza · Traditional Trattoria · Family Owned Since 2012

Family owned and operated on Duval Street since 2012, OnlyWood is one of the most authentically Italian restaurants in the Florida Keys. The story begins with an act of obsession: the owners imported a massive brick oven built entirely from volcanic materials sourced from Mount Vesuvius in Naples — capable of reaching 1,000 degrees, the temperature required for authentic Neapolitan-style pizza.

They make their own mozzarella following an ancient recipe. Their '00' flour and San Marzano tomatoes are imported directly from Italy. Homemade gnocchi, fresh fettuccine, hand-rolled lasagna. The kitchen does not exist to approximate Italian food — it exists to honor it.

Signature

Vesuvius volcanic stone oven · House-made mozzarella · Imported Italian ingredients · Chef/owner name to be confirmed

OnlyWood
Neapolitan Pizza · Trattoria
03

Little Pearl

Chef's Tasting Menu · Local Seafood · Wine Bar · Seven Nights

The crown jewel of the four in terms of prestige and narrative. Little Pearl is the creation of restaurateurs whose combined portfolio — including Italian mainstay Antonia's (celebrating over 25 seasons) and seafood destination Thirsty Mermaid — represents more than two decades of building Key West's serious dining culture, one dish at a time.

Little Pearl operates on pure chef's conviction: a tasting menu only, seven nights a week, three seatings. No substitutions. No dumbing down. The restaurant even produces its own wine — the Lange Little Pearl Cuvée Pinot Noir from Oregon's Willamette Valley — in direct collaboration with the kitchen. That is the level of intentionality at work here.

Signature

Four-course Chef's Tasting Menu only · Lange Little Pearl Cuvée wine · Three nightly seatings: 5pm, 7pm, 9pm · Chef/owner name to be confirmed

04

Bocado

Authentic Spanish Tapas · Created Expressly for This Space

Bocado is the most significant story of all four kitchens — because it did not exist before The Reserve Key West. The concept was created expressly for this space. A chef believed in this building, this vision, and this community enough to originate something entirely new.

Bocado is not a tenant. It is a creation. The Reserve Key West is its birthplace. This distinction is worth celebrating: one of our four kitchens exists because of this building. That is a rare and powerful thing to say — and it is true.

The Ledger

Main Service Bar · The Anchor · Adjacent to the Mural

A ledger is what a bank ran on — the book of accounts, the record of what was deposited, what was earned, what was spent. It is also the word for a flat stone laid horizontally, a surface you lean on.

The double meaning is accidental but perfect. The Ledger is the primary service bar, the workhorse, the anchor of the main floor. Adjacent to the 1962 mosaic mural wall. This is where the evening finds its rhythm.

Our Story

A building that has always held something valuable — first the savings of a community, now the culinary legacy of an island.

1962
The Origin
First Federal Savings & Loan

The Building with
a Past

In 1962, First Federal Savings & Loan opened at 510 Southard Street in Key West. The building was a statement of permanence and community trust — mid-century modernism at its most confident. The sculptural wave ceiling was poured. The mosaic mural depicting Key West history in turquoise, aqua, and cobalt was installed. The vault door was sealed.

First Federal wasn't just a bank. It was where Key West families got their mortgages. Where fishermen banked their season. Where a young Key West kept its future. The building predated the tourists, the commercialization, and the fame. It was built for the people who lived here.

The architectural details that still define the building — the wave ceiling, the mural, the vault door — were not decorative choices. They were a declaration. This was a place meant to endure. And it has.

"On this site since 1962. First Federal kept the island's savings. We keep its flavors." The Reserve Key West · 510 Southard Street
2026
The Rebirth
The Reserve Key West Opens

The Vault
Opens Again

The Reserve Key West opens the vault again. Four of the island's most celebrated chefs — each with decades of Key West history — take up residence in the same building that once held the community's savings.

The building that held financial reserves now holds something harder to measure: the culinary reserve of the Florida Keys.

Thai Island and MisoHappy bring a kitchen rooted in a garden she planted herself. OnlyWood carries a Vesuvius oven from Naples. Little Pearl brings the most lauded tasting menu format in the Keys. And Bocado — the newest of the four — was created expressly for this building. It did not exist before The Reserve. It was born here.

The Reserve Key West is not a restaurant. It is not a bar. It is not a food hall. It is a building that has always held something valuable.

The History

A Building with Memory

1962

First Federal Opens

First Federal Savings & Loan opens at 510 Southard Street. The building is completed — mid-century modernism at its most confident. The sculptural wave ceiling is poured. The vault door is installed and sealed. One of the most architecturally significant interiors in the Florida Keys.

The Mural

The Mosaic — Key West in Tile

The original mosaic mural depicts Key West history in turquoise, aqua, and cobalt. It is the color DNA of the entire venue — every design decision flows from its palette. The mural was never moved. It never needed to be.

2026

The Reserve Opens the Vault

The Reserve Key West LLC brings the building back to life as a chef-driven culinary landmark. Four independent kitchens. Three bars. The original vault door still in place. The building that held the community's savings now holds its culinary legacy.
